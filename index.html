Pointing Error Circle

Torque Budget (B1)

How to read: Stacked bars decompose the peak motor torque into its physical contributors — unbalance (gravity), friction, wind/drag, firing impulse, and acceleration (J·α). Each segment's height is that component's share of Tpeak/motor. Target: acceleration should dominate ≈ 40–70 % at peak demand; if friction or unbalance dwarfs J·α, the drivetrain is oversized for the dynamics. A tall red firing bar means recoil is driving peak sizing — check if it's a true design case or a rare event.

Efficiency Map η(T,ω) (B2)

How to read: Contours show motor efficiency η(T,ω) = Pmech/Pelec over the operating envelope. Each curve is an iso-efficiency line; the red cross marks the duty-cycle peak operating point (Tpk/motor, ωpk). Target: the cross should sit inside the high-η band (typ. 80–90 %). Sitting on a low-η contour means the gearing is mismatched — reduce/increase ratio, or pick a motor with a peak in that region.

Power – Speed Overlay (B3)

How to read: The blue curve is mechanical output power Pmech(ω) = T(ω)·ω along the motor torque-speed envelope; the orange curve is electrical input Pelec(ω) = Pmech/η. Their vertical gap is instantaneous dissipation. The red cross marks the duty-cycle peak operating point. Target: the cross should sit near the knee of the Pmech curve (max power region) with room below rated power; far from the knee means the motor/ratio pairing wastes either torque or speed headroom.

Duty Cycle FFT (B5)

How to read: Frequency-domain magnitude |T(f)| of the torque duty profile. Peaks at low frequencies are the fundamental slew and its harmonics; the dashed red line marks the mechanical bandwidth fm. Target: significant torque content should fall well below fm — energy above fm is what the drive cannot physically track, producing lag/overshoot. If a strong peak straddles fm, either raise drive bandwidth (stiffer gearing / higher ωM) or lower commanded profile aggressiveness.
